The Dawn of Galactic01: Virgin Galactic's Exciting Return to Space

Updated 13:25 UTC May 25th 2023

VMS Eve Mothership Credit : Virgin Galactic

As the curtain lifts on a new chapter in space exploration, all eyes are on Virgin Galactic. After a near two-year hiatus, the innovative company is set to launch its highly anticipated flight, Galactic01, at the end of June. This return to the cosmos is a testament to the company's resilience, having overcome numerous challenges and made significant improvements to their flight hardware and infrastructure.

Galactic01 also known as Unity23 mission's crew member. Credit : Virgin Galactic

In 2021, Virgin Galactic etched its name into the annals of history. Their first flight, a resounding success, transported crew members, including company founder Richard Branson, to the edge of space. Despite a minor deviation from the planned flight path, which attracted attention from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), the mission was hailed as a landmark achievement in space travel.

Fast forward to today, and we find ourselves on the precipice of another monumental journey. Galactic01, previously known as Unity 23, isn't just another trip to space; it's Virgin Galactic's fifth manned spaceflight and the first commercial mission of SpaceShipTwo. Commissioned as a research mission by the Italian Air Force, the flight aims to gather invaluable data that will further our understanding of space travel.

VSS Unity view from Space Credit : Virgin Galactic

The crew selected for this mission personifies expertise and bravery. Pilots Frederick Sturckow and Michael Masucci will command the vessel, supported by crew members Beth Moses, Walter Villadei, Angelo Landolfi, and Pantaleone Carlucci. From the sprawling expanse of Spaceport America in New Mexico, these astronauts will climb aboard VSS Unity, propelled to an altitude of 50,000 feet by the "mothership", VMS Eve, before their journey to the stars begins.

Each crew member has a unique role to play in this mission. Colonel Villadei, a seasoned space engineer and cosmonaut, will lead the mission, tending to rack-mounted payloads during the weightless segment of the flight. His smart suit will provide real-time data on his biometric and physiological responses. Lt. Colonel Landolfi will study cognitive performance in microgravity while Pantaleone Carlucci, an aerospace engineer, will wear sensors measuring heart rate, brain function, and other human performance metrics in the weightless environment. Finally, Virgin Galactic's Chief Astronaut Instructor, Beth Moses, will oversee the mission's smooth execution, guiding the crew through key mission phases and cabin instructions. Galactic01 has lofty goals. By studying the transitional phase from gravity to microgravity on the human body, and evaluating the effects of a microgravity environment on various chemical and physical properties, the mission aims to generate insights that will guide the design of future spaceflight systems and technologies. Despite the challenges, Virgin Galactic remains focused on its audacious plan of providing daily suborbital flights to the edge of space.
