Spacex STARSHIP SN8 update

Nov 17th 2020

Starship SN8 credit:@austinbarnard45

—

A few days ago, SpaceX carried out its third static fire test on its Starship SN8 prototype. The test saw the rocket as if it were about to launch, so the engines were turned on for a few seconds while the spacecraft remained attached to the ground. Unfortunately, unlike the other two static fire tests, this time there were problems.

About 2 secs after starting engines, martyte covering concrete below shattered, sending blades of hardened rock into engine bay. One rock blade severed avionics cable, causing bad shutdown of Raptor.

— Elon Musk (@elonmusk) November 17, 2020

As per Elon Musk: "About 2 secs after starting engines, martyte covering concrete below shattered, sending blades of hardened rock into the engine bay. One rock blade severed avionics cable, causing a bad shutdown of Raptor."

Starship SN8 3 raptor engines credit:Elon Musk

Starship SN8 prototype is powered by 3 raptor engines. During the last test, one of these thrusters was damaged. As per the last video update, a new Raptor engine SN42 has been installed to replace the damaged one.
